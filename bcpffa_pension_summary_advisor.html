04. Payroll Deductions Breakdown (2026 Rules)

CPP and EI premiums stop being deducted once you reach their respective annual income caps.

CPP

Canada Pension Plan (CPP & CPP2)

Base CPP (5.95% above $3.5k to $74.6k) $0.00 / $4,230.45
CPP2 (4% on $74.6k to $85.0k) $0.00 / $416.00

The CPP enhancement has a second bracket (CPP2) targeting high-middle earners. Once your earnings exceed $74,600, your base contribution stops and you contribute 4% on earnings up to $85,000.

EI

Employment Insurance (EI)

EI Premiums (1.63% up to $68.9k) $0.00 / $1,123.07
Rate: 1.63%
Max Insurable Limit: $68,900.00

EI premiums are applied at a flat rate of 1.63%. Deductions cap entirely once your annual income reaches $68,900.
